# ORCA VC Positioning Review Pack

## Task

Decide whether ORCA should lead its VC / B2B-facing pitch with a capital-allocation frame, a strategic-decision frame, or a consumer-demand intelligence frame.

The current owner question:

> Would ORCA be stronger if "capital allocation" became the main selling point, while durable / transient / manufactured demand, action ceilings, and evidence-backed public-signal judgment became supporting mechanisms?

Important nuance: "capital allocation" may sound higher-prestige in B2B and VC contexts, but it may also sound too finance-specific or imply portfolio construction / investment tooling. Please pressure-test that tradeoff.

## Constraints To Preserve

- Do not position ORCA as generic finance software, portfolio construction, social listening, trend analytics, a dashboard, a data broker, generic AI research, or a report factory.
- ORCA's current first proof wedge is US-market tractioned indie/DTC beauty or personal-care operators with live 30-90 day demand-allocation decisions.
- Consumer seed/VC fund screen is a warm second door, not the first proof ICP.
- Use "evidence-backed" for buyer-facing language; keep "source-backed" / provenance-verified as the audit mechanism underneath it.
- Do not claim buyer validation, willingness to pay, repeatability, commercial readiness, product readiness, or judgment-quality proof unless the included sources explicitly support it.
- Preserve the difference between the broad company thesis and the first proof wedge.

## Three Candidate Compressed Pitches

### Version 1 - Strategic Allocation Judgment

ORCA is an evidence-backed strategic allocation judgment system for consumer markets. It helps operators and investors decide when visible demand justifies committing budget, moving quickly, holding, or discounting noise before internal data is conclusive. The first proof wedge is beauty and personal care, where ORCA distinguishes durable, transient, and manufactured demand from messy public signals.

### Version 2 - Decision Evidence, Less Finance-Heavy

ORCA turns messy public market signals into evidence-backed decision decks for high-stakes consumer-market decisions. It helps teams choose whether to commit, move, hold, or discount before budget, inventory, retail, launch, or pricing bets are made. Beauty and personal care is the first proof wedge; durable / transient / manufactured demand judgment is the mechanism, not the whole category.

### Version 3 - Capital Allocation, But Bounded

ORCA helps consumer-market leaders make better capital-allocation decisions before their internal data catches up. It converts public demand evidence into a calibrated action ceiling: commit to durable demand, move on transient demand, hold when evidence is capped, or discount manufactured noise. The first proof wedge is beauty and personal care operators; later expansion can serve fund screens and diligence.

## Questions For Review

1. Which pitch is strongest for VC fundraising and why?
2. Is "capital allocation" too finance-coded, or does it elevate ORCA enough in a B2B/investor context?
3. Would "strategic allocation" or "resource allocation" be a better category phrase?
4. What is the best one-sentence pitch after improving the wording?
5. What should the first three slides say?
6. What wording should ORCA avoid because it overclaims proof or miscategorizes the product?

## Current Recommendation To Pressure-Test

Use capital allocation as the buyer-value layer, not the category label:

> ORCA is an evidence-backed strategic allocation judgment system for consumer markets.

Then explain:

> It helps teams decide whether visible demand justifies committing budget, moving quickly, holding, or discounting noise before internal data is conclusive.

This keeps the prestige of allocation language without making ORCA sound like generic finance or investment software.
