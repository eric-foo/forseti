## Commissioned Evidence Classes

For each class, perform the smallest attempt proving useful yield or the real blocker and preserve factual samples through Capture.

1. **Launch chronology/product age.** Seek first-party announcements, owned timestamps, retailer publication evidence, credible trade coverage, and archives. Record date type and ambiguity. Archive dates and earliest reviews are not launch dates alone.
2. **Review/rating/retail-traction history.** Capture current identity-stable Sephora and exact-official Amazon fields where available: rating, total, distribution, price, promotion, availability, variants, placement, Amazon bought-in-past-month band, bestseller rank. Seek comparable history. Otherwise return `CURRENT_BASELINE_CAPTURED__RECURRENCE_REQUIRED`.
3. **Portfolio investment/lifecycle.** Capture launch cadence, shelf expansion/contraction, variants, bundles, reformulation, campaign emphasis, retirement, discontinuation. Do not infer internal spend or profit directly.
4. **Channel/geography dependence.** Capture assortment overlap, exclusives, authorization, coverage, international listings, travel retail, and channel launch timing. Keep contexts separate.
5. **Cannibalization prerequisites.** No causal verdict. Test launch sequence, stable identities, before/after metrics, assortment movement, search, price/promotion, and category alternatives. State missing data and feasible cadence.
6. **Manufacturing/supply dependencies.** Sample packaging labels, manufacturer/distributor addresses, recalls, customs/import records, supplier disclosures, ingredient commonality, trademarks, relevant jobs. Shared ingredients/addresses do not prove supplier identity; jobs do not prove execution.
7. **Customer-acquisition leverage.** Sample ad libraries, campaign/creator activity, affiliate infrastructure, promotion cadence, reproducible branded-search history, and cross-franchise bundling. No representative channel share without a denominator.
8. **Competitive posture.** Select competitors only from retailer adjacency, explicit substitutions, or Summer Fridays evidence. Capture dated price, launch, claim, assortment, placement, and substitution evidence. No general competitor dossier.
9. **Material events/conflicts/shocks.** Search primary or credible reporting for recalls, litigation, controversy, boycotts, retailer conflict, regulatory action, leadership/ownership change, supply disruption, geopolitical exposure, and material partnerships. Capture the strongest original source or bounded searched-and-not-found; do not manufacture drama.
