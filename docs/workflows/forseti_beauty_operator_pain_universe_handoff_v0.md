## Commission

Enumerate the problem universe of US beauty brands at $10M-$150M revenue with
DTC + retail distribution — **starting from what operators actually face, not
from what is observable**. Sources: trade press, industry reports, operator
interviews/podcasts/postmortems, founder communities, retailer compliance
documentation, industry analysts. **Paid memberships and paid industry
sources are sanctioned for this pass (owner, 2026-07-18)** — silent pains by
definition under-appear on free surfaces; name every paid source used so the
receipt travels with the finding.

Then classify every enumerated pain into exactly one of:

- **observable** — a public surface shows it directly (candidate detection
  class; supply the signature);
- **corroborable** — public surfaces show a proxy or second teller (job
  postings, hiring patterns, dated announcements) but not the pain itself;
- **invisible** — no public falsifier exists (record it anyway: the invisible
  list is the honest boundary of the product, and conversation material for
  sell-to-learn calls).

For each observable candidate, run the five-gate admission rule (from the
taxonomy): identifiable internal owner; recurring expensive decision;
sub-hour manual public test; public falsifier; stated hard limit. Candidates
failing any gate go to corroborable or invisible, not into a weakened class.

## Claim discipline (binding on the output)

unproven hypothesis. Every class states its hard limit. Never characterize
legality, compliance, or fault from public evidence. No demand, pain-rate, or
"costs them $X" claims without a stated public basis.
