## Independent observations added at R4

- **OBS-009 — Allure multi-tester review:** corroborates creamy liner,
  blendability, and some durable wear. A different tester found one Born Star
  shade messy, weak in payoff, and migrated after roughly five hours; Heart On
  did not survive eating, drinking, or kissing. The tester set is small and
  product/shade-specific.
- **Independent origin — The Looker/Daily Beast:** discloses pre-launch product
  samples and reports buildable bronzer, durable blush/eyeshadow, and roughly
  8–10-hour mascara wear with easy removal. Sample provision is visible.
- **Independent origin — LiftBakeLove:** discloses self-purchase and reports
  roughly one-hour Joystick wear even when set, bulky/light packaging, Born
  Star online-shade mismatch, lingering Heart On taste, and Money Shot
  patchiness when worn alone.
- Repeated launch-copy coverage was not counted as independent product-
  performance corroboration.

## Cumulative claim boundary supported by the full packet

The current public evidence supports the existence of a coherent maximalist,
sensorial, tactile proposition across Coty, Marc Jacobs, and Sephora surfaces.
External evidence supports some product- and shade-specific alignment on
texture, blendability, and durability, while independently and repeatedly
showing conflicts in blush wear, packaging hand-feel, shade representation,
eyeliner performance, Heart On longevity/taste, and Money Shot condition or
texture. The evidence does not support a uniform line-wide performance verdict,
prevalence, defect rate, representative demand, delivery state, or local-stock
claim.
