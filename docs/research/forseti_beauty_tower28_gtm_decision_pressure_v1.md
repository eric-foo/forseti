## Claim Discipline

Every material claim cites a Phase 1 observation id (OBS-NNN) or is labeled
`hypothesis` (to be established in private confirmation, never asserted as
fact). Missing internal facts are named as boundaries, not filled with generic
hypotheses. Nothing here is buyer proof, pain proof, urgency proof, budget
proof, or willingness-to-pay proof; no outreach was performed.

## Reasoning Sequence (applied)

```text
Phase 1 observed company/customer reality
-> candidate decision surfaces (three compared below)
-> recurring problem hypothesis
-> accountable decision owner
-> concrete action and timing
-> consequence of error or delay
-> current substitute and suspected inadequacy
-> minimum private confirmation
-> reachability and budget-adjacent path
-> faithful same-decision proof route
-> recommend, hold, or reject
```

## Candidate Comparison (internal, capped at three)

### Candidate A — Assortment allocation and SKU intervention inside the expanding Sephora footprint

Observed basis, stated at its true strength: Sephora NA shelf footprint
reportedly doubled from 4 to 8 shelves across ~500 doors in 2026 (OBS-012 —
brand-supplied figures in a trade interview, one origin, unaudited); the line
spans 25 carried items including a seven-product SOS skincare family (OBS-008);
public reception at Sephora is dispersed — heroes rate 4.3–4.6 while the
mascara (3.82 across 2,953 reviews), pressed powder (3.71), highlighter (3.56),
and skin tint (3.98) sit below 4.0, with the hero SOS spray at 4.09 across
4,855 reviews (OBS-009 — aggregate reception proxies; no review text sampled,
no category baseline establishing what a sub-4.0 rating predicts). Dated
buyer-language titles exist: a mascara rejection with switching intent
(2026-05-31, OBS-018) — the one item that is both sub-4.0 and named in a
rejection title; a concealer rejection title (2026-06-06, OBS-017 — but Swipe
rates 4.34, not sub-4.0); a lipgloss separation question (2026-07-04, OBS-016 —
SKU not mapped to the sub-4.0 cluster). An organized lower-price substitution
narrative exists around the heroes (OBS-027 — dupe-page existence, explicitly
not switching volume). DTC shows shade-level sold-outs and one markdown
(OBS-004).

Candidate decision surface (hypothesis): with expanded shelf space and a
broadened makeup-plus-skincare line, someone at Tower 28 may repeatedly decide
which SKUs/franchises earn expanded placement, which get reworked, and which
get pulled. Whether that decision recurs as framed, who owns it, and how much
allocation agency the brand has versus Sephora are all unconfirmed.

### Candidate B — Claims-surface governance across retailer PDPs

Observed basis: a verified 2024 complaint quotes Sephora PDP copy
("non-comedogenic") against a named ingredient, with related concealer threads
recurring into 2025 (OBS-023); the brand's own ingredients page currently
avoids the word non-comedogenic while carrying seal and testing claims
(OBS-003); an unverified TikTok breakout/purging complaint theme, undated at
content level, sits against the sensitive-skin positioning (OBS-025); the brand
entered the SPF/OTC category where claims carry regulatory weight (OBS-007).

Why not leading: the anchor contradiction is over-180-day evidence; the current
Sephora PDP copy state was not re-read this pass; the complaint theme is
title-level and undated (CR-002 pending); no current regulatory or NAD event
exists (OBS-015). The trigger cannot be shown live rather than historical.

### Candidate C — Amazon channel-control formalization

Observed basis: brand-attributed GMV includes Amazon (OBS-012) while the
brand's own channel list omits Amazon (OBS-005) and international Amazon flows
carry ~60% price markups (OBS-011).

Why not leading: the decisive fact — who actually sells Tower 28 on amazon.com
and whether the brand controls it — is unresolved (GAP-004, CR-003).
Recommending a channel-control pattern on an unresolved contradiction would
manufacture pressure from a coverage gap. Revisit only after CR-003.

## Gate Adjudication — why no candidate clears

The commissioning packet distinguishes a clearing pattern (one that passes the
evidence, consequence, timing, substitute, reachability, recurrence, and
proof-path gates) from company understanding with candidate hypotheses.
Candidate A, the strongest, was tested gate by gate:

- **Evidence**: partially clears. A dated allocation-context event exists
  (OBS-012, brand-supplied) and reception dispersion is real (OBS-009), but the
  rejection/switching overlap with the sub-4.0 cluster is one SKU (the
  mascara), not a pattern across "the same items," and no review text or
  category baseline supports interpreting the dispersion as a problem.
- **Consequence**: class plausible, size unknown (GAP-007). The earlier claim
  that one retail relationship "carries the brand's US wholesale exposure" was
  an overreach: Sephora is the largest *observed* specialty-retail footprint,
  but channel revenue shares are unobservable and Amazon flows are unresolved
  (OBS-005, OBS-011, OBS-012).
- **Timing**: hypothesis only. No Tower 28/Sephora reset cadence was observed.
- **Substitute**: hypothesis only. What the company uses today is unknown.
- **Reachability**: not established. The founder is publicly visible and
  press-active (OBS-002, OBS-012), but no access route exists in evidence;
  "warm introductions" was an unsupported inference and is withdrawn. The
  earlier use of job-posting absence to support founder ownership of this
  decision exceeded the commission's org-motion proxy ceiling (OBS-006) and is
  withdrawn; owner identity is a pure hypothesis.
- **Recurrence**: hypothesis to test later across pool companies.
- **Proof path**: designed (sealed same-decision backtest, below) but
  contingent on the pattern being confirmed at all.

Since the discriminating gates — problem liveness, owner, agency, timing,
consequence size, substitute, reachability, budget adjacency — are exactly the
private-confirmation set, the honest state is: the substrate has produced a
strong, specific, testable hypothesis, not a clearing pattern.

## Hypothesis A — preserved confirmation route (not a recommendation)

If the owner authorizes confirmation discovery, the six questions that decide
whether a pattern exists:

1. Who decided the last SKU-level assortment/allocation change for Sephora,
   and what did the decision process look like? (ownership, agency)
2. How many such decisions recur per year, and what did the last mis-step cost
   (markdowns, returns, lost placement)? (recurrence, consequence)
3. What data and advisors are used for those calls today, and where did they
   fail last time? (substitute and its failure)
4. Did the sub-4.0 reception cluster (mascara, powder, highlighter, tint)
   change any action this year? (problem liveness)
5. Who pays today for decision support — analytics, agencies, retail
   consultants — and from which budget? (budget adjacency)
6. When is the next assortment-relevant window with Sephora? (timing)

Supporting design, all conditional on confirmation:

- **External evidence Forseti can already package**: reconciled reception
  dispersion with proxy ceilings (OBS-009), dated buyer-language titles
  (OBS-016 to OBS-018, OBS-023), substitution-narrative mapping (OBS-027),
  comparison-set context (OBS-026), claims-surface divergence (OBS-003 vs
  OBS-023), availability/markdown state (OBS-004). Decisive internal boundary:
  velocity, sell-through, returns, margin, and retailer scorecards are
  unavailable externally (GAP-007) — the Sprint form for this hypothesis is
  `ADMIT_WITH_AUTHORIZED_PRIVATE_CONTEXT`; if that bounded context is refused,
  the honest state is a hold, not a proxy.
- **Faithful same-decision proof route**: sealed backtest on a completed,
  observable assortment/SKU-intervention episode (a Tower 28 discontinuation,
  rework, or reposition identifiable from catalog history, or a pool-company
  equivalent) — predeclare frame and cutoff, reconstruct pre-cutoff public
  evidence only, seal the recommendation, then reveal the actual action. Case
  selection happens only after the decision family is confirmed.
- **Recurrence hypothesis**: scaling beauty brands with expanding
  specialty-retail footprints face recurring assortment-allocation decisions
  under dispersed public reception; test across other scaling-stratum pool
  companies before any hyperspecialization.
- **Kill tests**: retailer dictates assortment (no brand agency); existing
  retailer data plus dashboards answer these calls with low friction; sub-4.0
  reception provokes no internal concern; the expanded footprint's assortment
  was already locked. Reversal evidence: a hired merchandising/planning leader
  plus tooling; captures (CR-001..CR-003) or review sampling (REQ-004)
  contradicting the reception/complaint reads.
- **Coverage constraint carried from Phase 1**: community evidence is
  title-level in three routes and per-review text is unsampled; this
  hypothesis leans on aggregate dispersion and dated titles. CR-001 (thread
  bodies) and REQ-004 (bounded review-text sampling) would strengthen or
  weaken it materially before any confirmation conversation.
