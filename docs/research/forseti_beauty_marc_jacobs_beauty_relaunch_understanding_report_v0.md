## How To Read This Report

This is a one-company, decision-neutral Understanding report. It answers what
current public evidence shows about the relaunch proposition and where the
owned, retailer, and attributable experience surfaces align, conflict, or
remain unproven. It makes no demand, prevalence, purchase, competitor, or
internal-performance verdict. Coty is treated only as the publicly named
licensed operating partner. Community and review evidence establishes
attributable instances or bounded recurrence, never population rates or
representative demand.

The Executive Intelligence Brief is the decision-facing layer. Sections 3, 4,
9, and 10 retain the audit floor. Every load-bearing synthesis statement cites
the observation ledger and stays inside the acquisition seal's four accepted
residuals: partial exact owned-PDP capture, unpinned Sephora delivery/local
availability, nonrepresentative experience evidence, and an unverified
formula-equivalence inference.

## Executive Intelligence Brief

Marc Jacobs Beauty's observable relaunch promise is coherent at the expression
layer: maximalist self-expression, sensorial textures, playful tactile objects,
and long-wearing performance appear across Coty, Marc Jacobs, and Sephora
surfaces. The evidence supports the texture half more consistently than the
performance or luxury-object half. Creaminess, blendability, and buildability
recur across retailer reviews, community reports, and independent hands-on
testing; wear, shade representation, packaging hand-feel, and product condition
vary materially by product, shade, tester, and method (OBS-001–OBS-012).

**Front-page conclusions — claim / evidence bound / commercial consequence /
confidence / next observable:**

1. **The relaunch is propositionally coherent across the three observed
   official and retail layers, but that coherence proves expression, not
   performance.** Evidence bound: Coty defines a seven-product, $26–$42,
   sensorial and self-expression-led launch; current Marc Jacobs surfaces carry
   the same assortment and design language; Sephora translates it as playful,
   upgraded, multi-use, and long-wearing (OBS-001, OBS-002, OBS-003).
   Commercial consequence: informs whether the relaunch has a legible public
   proposition before product-level experience is considered. Confidence:
   high on observable expression; unproven on sales, demand, or internal launch
   performance. Next observable: whether the same seven-product architecture
   and claims remain stable after the initial launch window.

2. **Sensorial texture is the best-supported part of the promise; durable,
   uniform wear is not.** Evidence bound: smoothness, creaminess, blendability,
   and buildability recur across Sephora rows, Reddit threads, and three
   independent hands-on origins, while wear conflicts recur for Joystick,
   Born Star, Heart On, and some Drawn This Way shades (OBS-004–OBS-012).
   Commercial consequence: informs which part of the proposition currently has
   the strongest external corroboration and which claims require product- and
   shade-specific scrutiny. Confidence: corroborated for texture; moderate and
   conditional for wear because the evidence is small and heterogeneous. Next
   observable: a larger dated, non-incentivized negative-review sample by
   product and shade.

3. **Joystick's buildable, blendable use experience is corroborated, while its
   all-day implication and luxury-object hand-feel are directly contested.**
   Evidence bound: the captured Sephora sample is praise-weighted on blending,
   but a non-incentivized row reports light pigment and cheap, impractical
   packaging; independent and community users report roughly one-to-two-hour
   wear in some tests alongside longer favorable reports (OBS-004, OBS-007,
   OBS-010, OBS-011, OBS-012). Commercial consequence: informs claim and
   packaging-risk assessment for a visibly central launch item. Confidence:
   high that alignment and counterexamples both exist; prevalence unmeasured.
   Next observable: wear-keyed non-incentivized rows with application method and
   shade retained.

4. **Born Star has credible one-swipe and no-crease successes, but its
   long-wear claim is shade- and method-dependent and material condition
   complaints exist.** Evidence bound: positive rows and one independent
   review report easy application and all-day/no-crease wear; other rows and a
   tester report creasing, migration, weak pigment, shrinkage, or a product not
   fitting its pan (OBS-005, OBS-010, OBS-011, OBS-012). Commercial
   consequence: informs product-specific durability, shade-representation, and
   quality-control inquiry. Confidence: corroborated that mixed outcomes exist;
   no defect rate or line-wide conclusion is supported. Next observable:
   batch-, shade-, and fulfillment-aware review sampling.

5. **Heart On's moisturizing, smooth, buildable feel is repeatedly supported,
   while exact shade presentation and long-lasting/non-fading language are not
   uniformly supported.** Evidence bound: the captured rows praise comfort and
   application, but four substantive complaints cover photo mismatch, a loose
   cap, and two-to-three-hour wear; an independent tester also found the product
   did not survive eating, drinking, or kissing (OBS-006, OBS-010, OBS-012).
   Commercial consequence: informs the separation between sensorial product
   success and durability or digital-merchandising risk. Confidence:
   corroborated on existence of both alignment and conflict; prevalence
   unmeasured. Next observable: shade-specific return language and
   non-incentivized longevity rows.

**Choice-mechanism conclusions:**

- **Joystick:** the observed draw is dewy, skincare-like, buildable color; the
  experience often supports blending but sometimes fails on wear and luxury
  hand-feel; Rhode is a cited comparison, not a measured switching destination
  (OBS-004, OBS-007, OBS-010, OBS-011, OBS-012).
- **Born Star:** the observed draw is a one-and-done cream-to-powder eye look;
  ease and no-crease wear are supported for some shades and methods, while
  creasing, migration, low pigment, shrinkage, and bulky value concerns attack
  uniformity (OBS-005, OBS-010, OBS-011, OBS-012).
- **Heart On:** the observed draw is comfortable, buildable soft-shine color;
  texture aligns, while shade imagery, cap security, wear, and taste are
  conditional (OBS-006, OBS-010, OBS-012).

**Central-promise frame.** Observable value resides in a recognizable
maximalist, sensorial design system whose strongest external support is texture
and ease of use. That interpretation would weaken if current owned and Sephora
surfaces diverged or independent texture support disappeared. The evidence
shows the promise contested where it becomes absolute performance or tactile
luxury: wear, shade fidelity, packaging hand-feel, and product condition. That
threat would weaken if a larger dated sample showed those conflicts isolated to
rare handling or fulfillment events. This frame organizes evidence; it is not a
demand or recommendation judgment.

### 6. Strategic And Operating Chronology

Coty announced the licensed relaunch on May 20, 2026, with a seven-product
opening range, MarcJacobs.com and Sephora timing, Selfridges in the UK, and a
later Sephora-store rollout (OBS-001). By July 19, current Marc Jacobs and
Sephora surfaces showed the opening assortment live online (OBS-002, OBS-003).
The public sequence therefore supports announcement-to-online-retail execution
at the observed surfaces. It does not establish store-level availability,
sell-through, internal launch performance, or the completeness of geographic
rollout.

Two interpretations and their invalidation signals:

- **The relaunch uses a tightly coordinated expression system.** This is
  supported by repeated proposition and design language across Coty, owned, and
  Sephora surfaces (OBS-001–OBS-003). It would be invalidated by material
  divergence in current owned/retail positioning or assortment.
- **External experience is product- and shade-dependent rather than uniformly
  line-wide.** This is supported by contradictory results within Born Star,
  Drawn This Way, Joystick, and Heart On (OBS-004–OBS-012). It would be
  invalidated by a broader, reproducible sample showing the conflicts either
  disappearing or clustering around one common mechanism.

### 8. Competitor Context, Contradictions, And Gaps

Comparator references remain bounded observations. One Joystick reviewer
compares its skincare-like blending with Rhode; community discussion compares
Money Shot with Half Magic and Drawn This Way with cheaper liners. One user
infers formula or manufacturer equivalence from ingredient lists, but the seal
classifies that inference as unverified and this report does not adopt it
(OBS-004, OBS-007–OBS-009, OBS-012). No comparator base, switching volume,
portfolio-wide price comparison, or defensibility verdict was commissioned.

The strongest contradictions are product-specific:

- Joystick's blendability aligns, while all-day wear and luxury hand-feel do
  not align uniformly (OBS-004, OBS-007, OBS-011, OBS-012).
- Born Star ranges from no-crease longevity to creasing, migration, weak
  pigment, and material condition problems (OBS-005, OBS-010–OBS-012).
- Drawn This Way performance changes sharply by shade and use context
  (OBS-008, OBS-010).
- Heart On's sensorial feel aligns, while shade representation and absolute
  durability do not align uniformly (OBS-006, OBS-010, OBS-012).
- Money Shot has repeated attributable condition/texture complaints, but no
  defect rate, cause, or severe safety signal is established (OBS-009, OBS-012).

| Inferred | Retail shade-count differences are most plausibly assortment subsets; external performance is product/shade/method dependent; packaging hand-feel can undermine the intended tactile-luxury cue | Internal assortment strategy, causal defect mechanism, commercial impact |
