## Provenance and verification state

The source is a single external ChatGPT Pro response. Its method and internal
consistency were adjudicated against Forseti doctrine and the Tower 28
artifacts; its external citations (Schema.org GTIN exposure, GPC enforcement,
FDA directory caveats, certifier directories) were NOT dereferenced.
Verification rule: a class's citations and detectability are verified the
first time that class actually runs — no class is pitched to a prospect
before one real run of it survives.

## The core idea (decision-residue)

Hard internal decisions leave public fingerprints. A company never announces
"we are fighting about pricing" or "we can't decide whether to kill this SKU"
— but the shelf announces it: divergent effective prices, a retailer clearance
tag against a full-price brand page, an old formula still on sale a year after
the relaunch. These pains are invisible in reviews (customers don't complain
about master-data governance), which is exactly why they complement the
complaint-world evidence the CI report already collects.

## Admission rule for any future pain class (five gates, all required)

1. An identifiable internal owner (a titled executive who loses sleep over it).
2. A recurring, expensive decision it forces.
3. A sub-hour manual public test.
4. A public falsifier (a benign explanation you can check and exclude).
5. A stated hard limit (what public evidence can never prove).

The source's three rejected classes (contract-manufacturer strain,
paid-acquisition fatigue, returns burden) fail these gates and stay rejected.

## Claim discipline (binding for any use of this taxonomy)

- Three-layer packaging always: dated observation → bounded operational
  implication → unproven hypothesis; never collapsed.
- Every class's hard limit travels with any finding from it.
- Never characterize legality, compliance, or fault from public evidence.
- The GTM half of a class (owner, door-opener, pitch) never enters a CSB
  report; the report stays decision-neutral. Detection recipes are
  per-run scanning-handoff material, never CSB contract text.

## Tier A — runnable now, read-only public surfaces (owner-accepted)

For each: the pain in plain words / the public fingerprint / the hard limit
and internal owner.

1. **Same product, different facts (master-data divergence).** Nobody owns the
   authoritative product record, so the same barcode-identified product shows
   different sizes, names, ingredients, or images on the brand site vs Sephora
   vs Ulta. Check: five hero SKUs, strongest identifier (GTIN/UPC from package
   image or page markup), discrepancy grid across DTC + two retailers. Limit:
   cannot show which party caused it or what it costs. Owner: VP E-commerce /
   Digital Merchandising. Tower 28 brush: the SOS family's 7-products-under-8-
   catalog-tokens state.

2. **Three prices for one lipstick (price-pack conflict, observation half
   only).** DTC bundles, subscription discounts, and serial promos produce
   effective per-unit prices that undercut the brand's own retail shelf.
   Check: 30-day promo calendar from public campaign/newsletter archives +
   per-unit price ladder (DTC single / bundle / subscription / two retailers)
   on ordinary non-event dates, same size and market. Limit: cannot see
   redemption, realized price, margin, or retailer agreements. Owner: CFO /
   VP E-commerce. Owner ruling 2026-07-18: the cart code-testing step is
   dropped (see Parked).

3. **Retailer voting exit (markdown/delisting pressure).** A retailer's
   clearance state is the retailer's decision to stop reordering; persistent
   SKU-specific clearance while the brand site stays full-price means retailer
   and brand disagree about the product's future. Check: ten SKUs at the
   largest retailer, current price/status vs two archived dates, cross-checked
   against DTC state; exclude sitewide sale events. Falsifier: a quick
   restock or a successor SKU appearing means packaging/formula transition
   (class 5) or a planogram reset, not exit — persistence across dated
   observations is required. Owner note 2026-07-18: when this class fires on a
   tracked SKU, restock-or-not and time-to-restock is the follow-on question —
   that is longitudinal (co-movement seed), not a current point-in-time run.
   Limit: cannot see sell-through, who funds the markdown, or why.
   Owner: VP Sales / Key Accounts / CFO.

4. **Too many overlapping products (assortment sprawl).** Near-duplicate
   formulas under different names, retailer-only packs, and SKUs dead on the
   brand site but alive at retailers force recurring kill/keep/support
   decisions. Check: one category, enumerate current product URLs from sitemap
   + two retailers, group by formula/use/size/GTIN, compare with a 6-12 month
   older snapshot. Limit: cannot see SKU profitability or existing
   rationalization plans. Owner: CPO / VP Operations.

5. **Old and new versions both on sale (legacy-pack obsolescence).** Months
   after a redesign or reformulation, retailers still show or sell the old
   version — someone is sitting on legacy inventory. Check: establish the
   transition date from brand announcement/archive, inspect five retailers for
   old imagery, old ingredient text, or dual buyable versions on one UPC.
   Limit: cannot see units on hand, lot age, or whether sell-through is on
   plan. Owner: VP Operations / Regulatory.

6. **One retailer holds the keys (wholesale concentration).** Most observable
   doors, SKU breadth, and launch exclusives sit with one account. Already a
   CSB lens (Tower 28 CSC-004 is a live instance); the source's recipe upgrade
   — door-share calculated from public store locators — folds into that lens.
   Limit: observable distribution concentration only, never revenue
   concentration. Owner: CEO / CRO.

7. **Clean-badge exposure (formula-to-standard).** An ingredient in the
   current INCI list appears on an evolving retailer standard (Ulta Made
   Without, Credo Dirty List) or a state disclosure list — the badge or the
   shelf slot is at risk. Check: three hero formulas, transcribe INCI,
   normalize synonyms, map against the current and one archived version of the
   exact standard. Limit: cannot see concentrations, exemptions, or an
   in-flight reformulation. Owner: VP R&D / Regulatory / Key Accounts.

8. **SPF paperwork mismatch (regulated-SKU listing).** SPF, acne, and
   antiperspirant products are OTC drugs; the public FDA record (NDC
   Directory/DailyMed) can contradict the live label — lapsed marketing
   status, different actives, wrong labeler. Check: three regulated SKUs, full
   NDC from package imagery, compare label vs registry. Limit: FDA itself
   states the directory is incomplete and inclusion is not approval — a
   mismatch alone proves nothing illegal. Owner: Regulatory / Quality.
   Subject-applicable to SPF-carrying brands (Tower 28 included) — for those,
   this moves into the first checks.

9. **Logo without a directory entry (certification lapse).** A pack carries a
   certifier logo (Leaping Bunny, COSMOS, B Corp) that the certifier's own
   public directory doesn't back for that entity, product, or scope. Check:
   inventory logos on five hero products, search the certifier directories for
   brand, legal entity, and certificate code. Limit: directory lag, parent-
   company coverage, and licensees can explain a gap; cannot prove misuse.
   Owner: Sustainability / Regulatory / Brand Operations.

10. **Unauthorized sellers (gray-market diversion).** Owner-accepted
    2026-07-18; Forseti-added class (not in the source's 18; passes the five
    gates). The brand's authorized-retailer page names its channel; marketplace
    "sold by" seller lists show who actually sells. Every unauthorized unit
    leaked from somewhere in the chain — distributor dumping, retailer
    diversion, liquidation lots, arbitrage — and erodes price architecture,
    retailer parity, claims control, and recall ability. Check: authorized-
    retailer page vs Amazon/eBay seller lists for hero SKUs; count distinct
    third-party sellers and price vs MSRP. Falsifier: quiet authorization via
    a distributor or a first-party brand presence — the strong form is absence
    from the authorized list plus multiple independent sellers below MSRP.
    Limit: cannot see the leak's origin, volumes, or in-flight enforcement.
    Owner: CEO / General Counsel / VP Sales. Tower 28 instance (candidate,
    from an owner-supplied authorized-retailers screenshot omitting Amazon
    while Amazon carries product — must be re-observed in-lane before any
    report use; sharpens CSC-004).

## Tier B — accepted, lower priority for the $10-100M US bracket

- **Orphan web pages** (stale live PDPs, broken redirects): highly
  reproducible but the natural buyer is an SEO/digital lead — weakest fit for
  CI positioning; garnish, not a lead. Owner note 2026-07-18: some value if
  the pain is amplified in the framing (stale claims/prices still live, not
  just dead links).
- **Trademark collisions** (live opposition/refusal on a commercially live
  name): extends the entity/mark checks CSB already does; rare per subject.
- **Entity/IP misalignment** (mark owned by a dead or predecessor entity while
  the storefront names another): matters at financing/M&A moments; rare.
- **Launch slippage** (publicly moved dates, deleted teasers): weakest
  falsification in the set — too many benign explanations.
- **Supplier import refusals** (brand's named manufacturer in FDA refusal
  lists): accept-marginal; hardest to tie to the brand's own shipments.

## Parked (owner-deferred 2026-07-18; do not run without new owner word)

- **Retailer search/filter leakage** — owner ruling 2026-07-18: not pursuing
  ("let's not do this"); too fiddly to prove past personalization noise for
  the value it carries.
- **International variant fragmentation** — owner-deferred 2026-07-18; moot
  until subjects are multi-market.
- **Cart-interaction methods** — applying promo codes in a prospect's live
  checkout (class 2's code test; the strong form of affiliate/coupon
  leakage). Owner ruling: beyond read-only observation, and standalone
  commercial value is weak ("your coupon leaked" is not a report-selling
  pain). The read-only shred — a creator-exclusive code publicly indexed on
  coupon aggregators — may ride along as garnish only.
- **Privacy/adtech drift (GPC honoring)** — real pain, but detection needs
  browser network-capture instrumentation we have not bound, and the finding
  sits one sentence from a legal characterization we must never make. Owner:
  no monetization route identified. Deferred entirely.

## Rejected (stay rejected; source's own gates)

Contract-manufacturer concentration/MOQ strain; paid-acquisition
deterioration; returns/chargeback burden — none survive the five gates
(no public falsifier or no sub-hour test).

## How this taxonomy is consumed

- **GTM**: each Tier A class names whose inbox a finding belongs in — the
  door-opener for a defensive subject pitch or an offensive indie-buyer
  conversation. Ranking within Tier A is a heuristic prior, re-ordered per
  subject (SPF-heavy subject promotes class 8).
- **Commissioning**: when a company commission makes one of these pains
  decision-material, the commission names the job in CSB terms; the scanning
  handoff for that run carries that class's detection sketch. The menu feeds
  runs one dish at a time — never all 18 into the contract.
- **First-run verification**: the first run of each class verifies its
  citations and detectability before the class is ever used in outreach.
