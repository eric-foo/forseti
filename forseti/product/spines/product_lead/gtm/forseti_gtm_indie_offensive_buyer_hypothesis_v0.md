## The model

Every incumbent report has **two candidate buyer classes**:

1. **Defensive** — the subject itself (e.g., Tower 28): buys the report to see
   its own publicly visible exposure. Emotional valence risk: showing a brand
   its own flaws can trigger defensiveness.
2. **Offensive** — the subject's challengers: indie brands (< ~$10M) fighting
   the incumbent for shelf, positioning, or the dupe niche. The same chain
   card reads as attack intelligence ("the complaint that breaks their
   premium, their weak SKUs, their claims exposure"). Valence: delight, not
   defensiveness. Zero marginal production cost — the artifact already exists.

## Why not indie subjects (the two structural reasons)

- **Evidence starvation, not pain absence.** The method feeds on public
  footprint (review volume for the chain and proportionality, retail shelf
  state for concentration, trade press, a dupe ecosystem). A ~$3M brand has
  none at depth; a full-grade report is not buildable there at any effort.
  Partial footprint does exist on specialized retailers (Credo,
  Ministry-of-Scent-type shops — one such surface already captured in the
  search-surface MGT pilot), which is enough for prospect qualification,
  never for a subject report.
- **Different pain class.** Mid-bracket ($10-100M) pains are
  governance-of-what-exists (channel control, claims copy, assortment
  allocation, dupe defense). Indie pains are entry (which retailer to pitch,
  discovery, minimums, cash). Folding both into one shared-problem table
  would blur both segments.

## How indie pains get learned anyway

Indie pains are not publicly observable — but under the offensive-buyer route
they do not need to be. The incumbent report opens the conversation, and the
conversation is the pain research: sell-to-learn means each offensive pitch
doubles as the indie-segment pain interview, recorded into the shared-problem
table on the **buyer side** ("what would you pay to know about the incumbent
in your lane?").

## Prospect-map sources (targeting, not depth)

- The incumbent report's own substitution ecosystem names its attackers
  (Tower 28 report OBS-027: Prequel, ~50 listed dupes, the comparison-pair
  brands) — the report generates its down-market buyer list as a side effect.
- Specialized-retailer brand lists (Credo and analogues; the Capture spine's
  retailer-probe bound-target proposal should include such retailers partly
  because their catalogs double as indie directories).

## What confirms or kills this

Private-confirmation conversations ask BOTH buyer classes about the same
artifact. Confirm: an offensive buyer pays (any amount) or clearly states
willingness with a named decision it informs. Kill: repeated offensive
conversations yield interest without any decision-linked willingness, or
reveal that challengers only want entry intelligence our artifact does not
carry. If enough offensive conversations converge on the same entry-decision
pains, THAT table — not guesses — justifies designing an indie-specific
product later.

## Unchanged posture (for orientation, owned elsewhere)

next pool runs build the shared-problem table; ICP binding remains an
owner-made amendment to the GTM contract after that table exists. First
outreach targets are the companies whose reports we hold (both buyer classes
per report), not the bracket at large.
