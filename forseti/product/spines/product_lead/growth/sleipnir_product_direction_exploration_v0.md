## The central adjudication — what Sleipnir is, and what it must not silently become

**Finding (the load-bearing one).** The parent consumer-demand thesis *already contains*
the thing Sleipnir names. The offer hypothesis (`forseti_offer_hypothesis_v0.md`) already
defines a **broad decisions-as-a-service offer** — the "**Strategic Decision Evidence
Deck**" — that turns messy public market signals into **bounded, executive-grade CI
decision artifacts** for *any* consequential decision made before internal data is
conclusive (its own decision-families list includes category entry, positioning,
competitor-narrative response, and **founder/investor allocation decisions**). And the
thesis's **buyer ladder is already** operator → consumer-fund screen → **PE/family-office
diligence destination** — the "bigger wallets." Today that broad offer and its upper rungs
sit **deliberately behind** the beauty-consumer-demand first-proof wedge, unopened, with
the no-buyer-contact-before-full-spine-MVP outreach gate **closed**.

So "Sleipnir = sell CI decisions directly, up the ladder" is **not new product
substance.** It is a **product-identity + go-to-market + competitive-positioning
decision**: whether to give "the engine's **output** sold as general research/CI decision
artifacts to bigger-wallet / cross-vertical buyers, competing with incumbent CI/research
firms" its own compounding product identity, first wedge, and (eventually) charter — **the
exact move Aphrodite made** for the creator-signal surface. The parent thesis always
*contained* creator signal as a possible application; Aphrodite made it a named, bounded,
compounding product. Sleipnir proposes the same move for the engine's **core/general** CI
*service* surface.

**Therefore Sleipnir is a SIBLING productization of the shared engine, at the same
altitude as Aphrodite** — not a parent of it, not a supersession of the consumer-demand
thesis. Structurally the engine's general CI capability is the *superset*; Aphrodite is one
*vertical application*; but as go-to-market **lanes**, Sleipnir and Aphrodite are
**siblings**, each a bounded productization that sells the engine's *output*, not the
engine.

**Three things Sleipnir must NOT silently become** (drift guardrails, each tied to a real
boundary):

1. **Not a sale of the engine (golden goose).** It sells *decisions/research output* —
   never the engine, the registry/corpus, the method, controlled bulk data, or a raw feed.
   Selling those converts position-rent into a one-time sale and arms the buyer to not need
   you (growth-map §E). The only right "sell the engine" is at acquisition.
2. **Not a re-sequencing.** It must not quietly *open* the parent's deferred fund/diligence
   rungs *now*. That would jump the operator-first proof sequencing and the **closed
   outreach gate** (`advisory_proof_slice_definition_v0.md`), both of which Sleipnir
   **inherits, does not reopen**. Opening outreach is a separate owner decision.
3. **Not a superset thesis.** It must not demote the consumer-demand thesis to "one
   application under Sleipnir." The consumer-demand thesis stays the **ratified center** and
   the **first-proof lane in motion**; Sleipnir is a parallel cashing of the same engine,
   never its parent.

**Honest sequencing tension (owed to the owner).** Productizing the *general* CI service
before the *first* wedge (beauty operator) has produced **any** buyer proof — no buyer
contact yet; everything at `product_learning` — is a real risk: it is precisely the
premature scope-widening the parent thesis and buyer-proof packet guard against.

**Recommendation carried into every section below.** Adopt Sleipnir as a **named sibling
frame to hold and prepare**, but (a) tie its proof to the **same published-backtest
motion** the parent already runs, generalized past beauty; and (b) treat its highest-value
buyer (diligence / evidence-buyer) as **destination, not first door** — inheriting the
parent's discipline rather than racing ahead. This keeps a high-ceiling option compounding
without spending scope or outreach prematurely, and without ever selling the engine.

## Q1 — What is the product?

**Frame.** A **research / competitive-intelligence *service*** that sells **bounded, paid
decision artifacts** produced by the engine — the *decided answer on one named live
decision*, with an explicit action ceiling (monitor / probe / commit / hold / scale / avoid
/ reduce), what public signal can and cannot support, and what would change the answer. The
**engine stays internal** (golden goose); the buyer receives the *decision*, not the
engine, registry, method, or feed. It is the decision-memo / Vetting-Sprint *shape*,
generalized past the consumer-demand read to the engine's full stated scope (outside-in
**market & competitive** intelligence, not only own-brand demand).

**Competitive positioning (the "compete with CI firms" frame).** Sleipnir plays in the
**CI / research-services category** against incumbent providers — battlecard/CI platforms,
market & expert-research / financial-intelligence providers, and boutique CI/diligence
shops (landscape named as *illustrative context, not researched this turn* — public-info
claim discipline applies). The differentiation is the thesis's stated moat, applied
competitively: incumbents largely sell **reports, feeds, dashboards, or access to
sources/experts**; Sleipnir sells the **calibrated decision with an action ceiling +
published outcome-memory (backtest) receipts + honesty-as-moat (per-number provenance,
explicit missingness)**, with the engine as the un-sold source of that judgment. "Don't
sell the plumbing (or the report/feed) — sell the calibrated decision."

**What generalizes vs what is satellite** (the core/satellite split the packet already
owns):
- **Core (repeatable):** the grammar — bind one live decision + owner + deadline +
  consequence → capture provenance-hashed public signal → integrity-label
  (artificial-amplification / incentive distortion / copied-coordinated) → classify →
  entity-resolve (brand→parent / market→competitor) → calibrated action ceiling → memo +
  evidence appendix substrate → executive deck surface → outcome-memory receipts.
- **Satellite (adapts):** the specific *read* (competitive positioning, category-entry,
  competitor-move response, market-trajectory, deal-conviction — not only demand
  durability), the vertical, and the venue set. The parent's **demand-substrate hard gate
  is itself a satellite** of the demand read and **does not transfer** to a non-demand
  read; a Sleipnir read carries its own substrate gate, derived at design time.

**First sellable unit (offer hypothesis).** A fixed-scope **paid Decision Sprint** — one
qualified live outside-in decision, one bounded decision artifact (internal memo + evidence
appendix substrate, surfaced as the executive **Strategic Decision Evidence Deck**), and a
readback with the decision owner. Same fixed-scope-paid shape as the parent's first-offer
hypothesis and Aphrodite's Vetting Sprint, generalized. Primary output is
willingness-to-pay / decision-use evidence; revenue is secondary. **Not** SaaS, library
access, a dashboard, a feed, a data/registry dump, or expert-network access. Commercial
frame (price, cadence, sprint-vs-retainer) is **deferred** to a later owner-gated pass —
identical to the parent (`Commercial Frame Status`) and Aphrodite (register D-2).

**Reading — the "competitive intelligence" framing changes the picture.** When the sold
read was *own-brand consumer demand*, the "internal data isn't conclusive" premise
**weakened** as the buyer got bigger/data-richer (which is why the buyer-proof packet
defers retailer/category teams). But a **competitor / market read is about *others*** —
and **no buyer has their rival's or a target company's internal data**, so the outside-in
premise **holds even for large, data-rich buyers.** That reframing **broadens and
strengthens** the viable buyer set toward exactly the buyers incumbent CI firms already
serve (W2) and the highest-value outside-in buyer (W3).

**Recommendation (prepared, not decided):**
- **Anchor buyer / north star = W3 (diligence / evidence-buyer).** Highest structural value
  and clearest differentiation (buyer is entirely outside the target). Reached as
  **destination, not first door** — same call the parent already made.
- **Most-reachable paid CI buyer = W2 (corporate CI / strategy team)** for *competitor/market*
  reads — the segment CI firms are already paid for, where the outside-in premise survives
  scale. This is the natural head-to-head-with-incumbents wedge; its cost is an entrenched,
  enterprise-sales-motion market.
- **First *reachable* motion ≠ opening W2/W3 cold.** Reachability + the closed outreach gate
  + operator-first sequencing all bind. So the first Sleipnir *motion* is the **same
  published zero-spoiler backtest door-opener** the parent runs, **generalized past beauty
  to a competitor/market read** — proving in public (at `product_learning`) that the engine
  calls **competitive/cross-vertical** decisions, with **no new outreach**. That published
  proof is what earns the right to open a W2/W3 conversation.
- **W4 (horizontal) is a distinct axis, not a rung** — it widens the vertical, not the
  wallet; per the thesis, a later owner decision. Keep it separately gated.

*Open sharpening for owner sign-off:* "compete with CI firms" spans both the **corporate-CI
end** (W2 — battlecard/strategy buyers) and the **investor/diligence end** (W3 — market &
financial-intelligence buyers). The recommendation above anchors on W3 and stages toward W2;
the owner may prefer to lead with W2 (more established paid CI demand) at the cost of a more
crowded incumbent field.

## Q4 — Parent-boundary interactions (org-level-only default + forbidden set)

**The safe core needs no widening.** Sleipnir's clean lane is **org-level** outside-in CI —
reads about *companies, brands, markets, categories, competitors, demand* — sold as
decision artifacts, **public-info-only**, **honesty-as-moat**, **no outreach**, **no
person-level dossiers**, **no registry/corpus dump**, **never a feed**. That entire lane
sits **inside** the parent thesis's org-level-only default and forbidden set. This is the
default posture and the recommended scope.

**Each pressure point below is an EXPLICIT OWNER DECISION with the tradeoff named — not
assumed.** The A1 carve (Aphrodite creator-level) is the precedent for *how* a widening is
granted: narrow, bounded, owner-decided, tradeoff stated — never silent.

1. **Person-level reads.** Forbidden by default — and the CI-firm competitive frame creates
   *specific* pull here, because incumbents *do* sell person-level surfaces (exec/management
   tracking, expert-network calls). A diligence buyer wanting "assess this founder/management
   team," or a CI buyer wanting a rival's named execs, is **person-level**. Aphrodite carved
   *creator*-level; Sleipnir going *person*-level would be a **further, broader** carve.
   **Tradeoff:** it spends the honesty/neutrality moat and adds reputational/privacy risk.
   **Default: stay org-level** (read the *company's* org-motion, not the *person*). Route any
   such pull to the owner.
2. **Selling the corpus / registry / bulk data or query access** → the **golden-goose
   line** (§E): sell *derived* reads/decisions, never the dump or bulk access (the dump is
   the lead-list/person-directory the forbidden set already prohibits; it also arms the
   buyer to leave). Controlled query access is itself an owner decision, not a default.
3. **New venues / surfaces** beyond the capture lane's current bindings → a **capture-lane
   decision** under the measured-ToS-risk posture (absurd-level rejected), **never a
   per-deal stretch**.
4. **Outreach / lead-generation** ("who do we contact / build me a list") → the **forbidden
   outreach surface**; that is the Aphrodite-**Studio** separate-company shape, not
   Sleipnir. Sleipnir sells decisions, never contact.
5. **Feed / monitoring drift** → the **never-a-feed invariant** carries over. CI buyers pull
   hard toward "just give us a dashboard/feed" (it is the incumbent model); that is the
   buyer-proof **monitoring-only kill**. Recurring engagement is sold as recurring
   *decisions*, never a monitoring feed.
