## The central adjudication — what Sleipnir is, and what it must not silently become

**Finding (the load-bearing one).** The parent consumer-demand thesis *already contains*
the thing Sleipnir names. The offer hypothesis
(`forseti_offer_hypothesis_v0.md`) already defines a **broad offer** — the
"**Strategic Decision Evidence Deck** for foundational strategic and hyper-competitive
decisions" — that turns messy public market signals into bounded, executive-grade CI
decision artifacts for *any* consequential decision made before internal data is
conclusive (its own decision-families list includes category entry, positioning,
competitor response, and **founder/investor allocation decisions**). And the thesis's
**buyer ladder is already** operator → consumer-fund screen → **PE/family-office
diligence destination** — the "bigger wallets." Today that broad offer and its upper
rungs sit **deliberately behind** the beauty-consumer-demand first-proof wedge, unopened,
with the no-buyer-contact-before-full-spine-MVP outreach gate **closed**.

So "Sleipnir = the engine sold directly, up the ladder" is **not new product substance.**
It is a **product-identity + go-to-market decision**: whether to give "the engine sold
directly as general CI decision artifacts to bigger-wallet / cross-vertical buyers" its
own compounding product identity, first wedge, and (eventually) charter — **the exact move
Aphrodite made** for the creator-signal surface. The parent thesis always *contained*
creator signal as a possible application; Aphrodite made it a named, bounded, compounding
product. Sleipnir proposes the same move for the engine's **core/general** CI surface.

**Therefore Sleipnir is a SIBLING productization of the shared engine, at the same
altitude as Aphrodite** — not a parent of it, not a supersession of the consumer-demand
thesis. Structurally the engine's general CI capability is the *superset*; Aphrodite is
one *vertical application*; but as go-to-market **lanes**, Sleipnir and Aphrodite are
**siblings**, each a bounded productization of one engine.

**Two things Sleipnir must NOT silently become** (drift guardrails, each tied to a real
boundary):

1. **Not a re-sequencing.** It must not quietly *open* the parent's deferred fund/diligence
   rungs *now*. That would jump the operator-first proof sequencing and the **closed
   outreach gate** (`advisory_proof_slice_definition_v0.md`), both of which Sleipnir
   **inherits, does not reopen**. Opening outreach is a separate owner decision, exactly as
   for the parent lane.
2. **Not a superset thesis.** It must not demote the consumer-demand thesis to "one
   application under Sleipnir." The consumer-demand thesis stays the **ratified center** and
   the **first-proof lane in motion**; Sleipnir is a parallel cashing of the same engine,
   never its parent. (The handoff and the thesis both forbid supersession.)

**Honest sequencing tension (owed to the owner).** Productizing the *general* engine
surface before the *first* wedge (beauty operator) has produced **any** buyer proof — no
buyer contact yet; everything at `product_learning` — is a real risk: it is precisely the
premature scope-widening the parent thesis and buyer-proof packet guard against.

**Recommendation carried into every section below.** Adopt Sleipnir as a **named sibling
frame to hold and prepare**, but (a) tie its proof to the **same published-backtest
motion** the parent already runs, generalized past beauty; and (b) treat its

inheriting the parent's discipline rather than racing ahead. This keeps a high-ceiling
option compounding without spending scope or outreach prematurely.

## Q1 — What is the product?

**Frame.** "Research / CI as a service" = the outside-in market & competitive intelligence
engine sold as **bounded, paid decision artifacts** — the *decided answer on one named
live decision*, with an explicit action ceiling (monitor / probe / commit / hold / scale /
avoid / reduce), what public signal can and cannot support, and what would change the
answer. It is the **Vetting-Sprint / decision-memo *shape*, generalized** past the
consumer-demand read: the same grammar Aphrodite productized for creators and the parent
productized for beauty demand, applied to the engine's core general CI surface.

**What generalizes vs what is satellite** (the core/satellite split the packet already
owns):
- **Core (repeatable):** the grammar — bind one live decision + owner + deadline +
  consequence → capture provenance-hashed public signal → integrity-label
  (artificial-amplification / incentive distortion / copied-coordinated) → classify costly
  behavior → entity-resolve (brand→parent / market→competitor) → calibrated action ceiling
  → memo + evidence appendix substrate → executive deck surface → outcome-memory receipts.
- **Satellite (adapts):** the specific *read* (competitive positioning, category-entry,
  market-trajectory, deal-conviction — not only demand durability), the vertical, and the
  venue set. The parent's **demand-substrate hard gate is itself a satellite** of the
  demand read and **does not transfer** to a non-demand read; a Sleipnir read carries its
  own substrate gate, derived at design time.

**First sellable unit (offer hypothesis).** A fixed-scope **paid Decision Sprint** — one
qualified live outside-in decision, one bounded decision artifact (internal memo +
evidence appendix substrate, surfaced as the executive **Strategic Decision Evidence
Deck**), and a readback with the decision owner. Same fixed-scope-paid shape as the
parent's first-offer hypothesis and Aphrodite's Vetting Sprint, generalized. Primary
output is willingness-to-pay/decision-use evidence; revenue is secondary. **Not** SaaS,
library access, a dashboard, a feed, or a data dump. Commercial frame (price, cadence,
sprint-vs-retainer) is **deferred** to a later owner-gated pass — identical to the parent
(`Commercial Frame Status`) and Aphrodite (register D-2).

**Reading.** The **value-sharpest, most discriminating** buyer is **W3 (diligence /
evidence-buyer)** — precisely because outside-in CI is not a "nice increment" there but the
*only available read* (the investor is outside the company, has no internal panel, faces
the largest consequence, and pays for diligence edge). This is *why the parent thesis makes
it the destination.* Both weaker "brand-side" wedges (W1, W2) trend the wrong way: the more
scaled/enterprise the buyer, the **stronger their internal data**, which *weakens* Forseti's
"decide before internal data is conclusive" premise — the same reason the buyer-proof packet
**defers retailer/category teams**. W1 additionally **collides with Aphrodite's own §B
ladder** (indie → challenger/scale-up → big brands), so it is a poor sibling boundary.

**Recommendation (prepared, not decided):**
- **Anchor buyer / north star = W3 (diligence / evidence-buyer).** Name it as Sleipnir's
  ceiling and clearest differentiation — same call the parent already made (diligence =
  destination).
- **First *reachable* move ≠ opening W3 cold.** Reachability + the closed outreach gate +
  operator-first sequencing all bind. So the first Sleipnir *motion* is the **same
  published zero-spoiler backtest door-opener** the parent runs, **generalized past the
  demand read / past beauty** to at least one adjacent outside-in decision class — proving
  in public (at `product_learning`) that the engine calls **non-demand / cross-vertical**
  decisions, with **no new outreach**. That published proof is what later earns the right
  to open a W3 conversation.
- **W4 (horizontal) is a distinct axis, not a rung** — it widens the vertical rather than
  the wallet, and per the thesis is "a later owner decision, not implied by ratification."
  Keep it as an explicit, separately-gated option, not folded into the wallet ladder.

This mirrors the parent's own discipline exactly: destination named, first door reachable,
outreach gated, proof published — Sleipnir **inherits** it rather than jumping it.

## Q4 — Parent-boundary interactions (org-level-only default + forbidden set)

**The safe core needs no widening.** Sleipnir's clean lane is **org-level** outside-in CI —
reads about *companies, brands, markets, categories, competitors, demand* — sold as
decision artifacts, **public-info-only**, **honesty-as-moat** (per-number provenance,
explicit missingness, published backtests including misses), **no outreach**, **no
person-level dossiers**, **no registry dump**, **never a feed**. That entire lane sits
**inside** the parent thesis's org-level-only default and forbidden set. This is the
default posture and the recommended scope.

**Each pressure point below is an EXPLICIT OWNER DECISION with the tradeoff named — not
assumed.** The A1 carve (Aphrodite creator-level) is the precedent for *how* a widening is
granted: narrow, bounded, owner-decided, tradeoff stated — never silent.

1. **Person-level reads** (e.g., a diligence buyer wanting "assess this founder/management
   team"). Forbidden by default. Aphrodite carved *creator*-level; Sleipnir going
   *person*-level (execs/founders) would be a **further, broader** carve. **Tradeoff:** it
   spends the honesty/neutrality moat and adds reputational/privacy risk. **Default: stay
   org-level** (read the *company's* org-motion, not the *person*). Route any such pull to
   the owner.
2. **New venues / surfaces** beyond the capture lane's current bindings → a **capture-lane
   decision** under the measured-ToS-risk posture (absurd-level rejected), **never a
   per-deal stretch**.
3. **Outreach / lead-generation** ("who do we contact / build me a list") → the **forbidden
   outreach surface**; that is the Aphrodite-**Studio** separate-company shape, not
   Sleipnir. Sleipnir sells decisions, never contact.
4. **Selling the raw corpus/registry** → same as growth-map §E no-registry-dump: sell
   *derived* reads/decisions or controlled query access, never the dump (the dump is the
   lead-list/person-directory the forbidden set already prohibits).
5. **Feed / monitoring drift** → the **never-a-feed invariant** carries over. Diligence/CI
   buyers pull hard toward "just give us a dashboard/feed"; that is the buyer-proof
   **monitoring-only kill**. Recurring engagement is sold as recurring *decisions*, never a
   monitoring feed.

  substrate; executive Strategic Decision Evidence Deck as the buyer surface) → readback →
  pull-vs-praise classification. Same fixed-scope paid Decision Sprint as Q1.
- **Door-opener:** published zero-spoiler backtest receipts at `product_learning` (all

  decision class — the **shared** public-proof motion — plus Aphrodite's accruing proof as
  a cross-vertical demonstration. **No new outreach**; the standing gate stays closed.
- **Kill / park (adapted from the packet + Aphrodite §5):** monitoring-only / feed-or-
  dashboard pull → **kill**; consulting-risk-only (bespoke, non-repeatable) delivery →
  **park**; trust-refusal → disqualify; the public-signal increment **cannot beat the
  buyer's internal data** (the W2 big-brand risk) so the read is not decision-grade →
  park/kill; no fixed-scope paid pull after a useful artifact → kill.
- **Graduation anchor (mirrors Aphrodite):** ≥2 independent qualified decision owners at
  Grade A/B + ≥1 paid-sprint-level pull, **plus** the packet's repeatability /
  no-dashboard-no-source-system / no-bespoke-value / non-claim criteria still holding. This
  is the minimum repeat/pull anchor, not the full gate.
- **Claim tier:** everything capped at `product_learning` until receipts exist; the
  claim-defense wording discipline ("built to," never unreceipted "proven at"; tier label
  on every external sentence) applies to any published Sleipnir content.
