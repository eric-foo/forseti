## Product Job

Inside the creator registry, this panel answers the buyer’s money question:

> Why should this specific brand hire this specific creator for this specific
> campaign, what should the creator be hired to accomplish, and where would that
> spend be a mistake?

The surrounding registry may show identity, aggregate influence, cadence,
momentum, sponsorship history and reception, brand adjacency, rates or rate
context, freshness, and other separately owned facts when available. This
contract does not duplicate or own those panels. It owns the audience
triangulation and its commercial creator-fit projection within that complete
buying surface.

## Three Claims, Kept Distinct

| Claim layer | What it may say | Evidence basis | What it must not silently become |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Content-fit / intended-audience read** | Who the creator’s public content is pitched at or best fit for | Creator content, captions, transcripts, descriptions, recurring topics and formats | Mind-reading of creator intent; actual-audience census |
| **Observed participating-audience read** | What captured commenters reveal through language, questions, objections, jokes, rituals, category fluency, and elevated reactions | Captured top-level comments plus source-bound engagement and capture context | All followers, silent viewers, platform-wide prevalence, individual-level dossier |
| **Commercial creator-fit projection** | What a named buyer should hire the creator to accomplish, the product meaning and mechanism, strongest campaign jobs, briefing instructions, and anti-fit boundary | Judgment synthesis over the first two layers plus cited counterevidence and material registry facts | Guaranteed performance, conversion, ROI, or a source-free agency endorsement |

“Ideal audience” remains a useful buyer label, but it is not one undifferentiated
claim. The surface must expose the distinction above in its evidence drawer even
when the first-screen copy compresses it into one commercial profile.

## Layer Ownership

1. **Capture/Bronze** preserves source-faithful creator content, top-level
   comments, source-visible metrics, source identity, timestamps, and capture
   position or truncation facts.
2. **Silver or equivalent mechanical projection** may compute and carry dated,
   source-bound facts such as comment likes, video likes/comments, ratios,
   within-video rank/percentile, capture position, duplicate clusters, and exact
   lineage. It may assemble a creator-isolated evidence packet. It does not
   decide what the audience is or what a brand should buy.
3. **Cleaning/ECR** may normalize, deduplicate, label semantic evidence
   candidates, and preserve non-destructive transformation lineage. It does not
   make the fused hiring conclusion.
4. **Judgment** owns content/comment interpretation, agreement, contradiction,
   missingness, evidentiary salience, and the fused commercial creator-fit claim
   set. Engagement changes salience, not truth status.
5. **Creator Signal** owns the maximally commercial presentation of accepted
   claims inside the registry: ordering, buyer language, briefing instructions,
   spend-protection boundaries, evidence-drawer access, and claim withholding.

A packet named “Gold-ready” or similar is still an evidence handoff; its name
does not move subjective audience conclusions into Silver.

## Evidence And Inference Rules

- Use all admissible captured top-level comments from the selected videos after
  source-faithful preservation; selection and diversity controls may affect
  model context, but must not rewrite the capture as a platform census.
- State the selected-video and comment-capture posture. Engagement-ranked,
  page-limited, top-video, or otherwise truncated capture remains valuable and
  biased at the same time.
- Bind comment and video engagement metrics to their observed timestamps.
  Cross-date ratios or comparisons must be labeled as such or withheld.
- Comment likes, within-video rank/percentile, and saturated absolute likes may
  raise salience. Comment likes divided by video comment count is a useful
  attention comparison only when its dimensional meaning and timestamp posture
  are visible; it is not an audience-share estimate.
- A highly engaged joke or reaction may prove culture, language, community
  resonance, presentation-style fit, participation, or memorability. It does not
  alone prove shopping intent, price posture, product affinity, or demographics.
- Deduplicate repeated phrases, cap one-video dominance, and retain
  counterevidence so one joke, giveaway response, pinned comment, or breakout
  video cannot silently define the creator.
- Never mix creators inside one inference context unless an accepted isolation
  mechanism makes cross-creator evidence leakage impossible.

## Maximum-Defensible-Aggression Rule

> Write the strongest commercially useful sentence the evidence supports.
> Never weaken a supported claim merely to sound cautious. Never strengthen it
> past the evidence ceiling.

Buyer-facing copy may confidently use causal or outcome-oriented commercial
rhetoric when the underlying claim chain makes the direction credible. Allowed
forms include:

- “generates observed engagement”;
- “makes products memorable”;
- “the obvious next bottle”;
- “the commenters her videos attract”;
- “protect it and it pays you back”;
- “the one people argue about”;
- “strongest fit,” “best suited to,” and “wrong hire for.”

These phrases are commercial synthesis, not new measurements. Each must resolve
through claim IDs to the evidence and limitations that make the sentence
defensible. The surface does not need academic hedging or inline citations in
every sentence when that binding is one action away in the evidence drawer.

Still forbidden:

- fabricated numbers, probabilities, lift, conversion, or ROI;
- a guarantee of sales, conversion, campaign performance, or creator quality;
- “most,” “majority,” platform-wide prevalence, or demographic assertions from
  captured comments alone;
- unqualified actual-audience claims or individual-level sensitive attributes;
- aggressive copy whose supporting claim is withheld, stale, or absent.

## Buyer-Facing Panel Grammar

Above the evidence drawer, use this order:

1. **Hire verdict** — `Hire <creator> when <campaign job>.`
2. **Product meaning** — what the creator makes the buyer’s product mean.
3. **Mechanism** — the repeatable creative machine the brand is renting and the
   observed engagement or audience response it generates.
4. **Strongest campaign jobs** — concrete briefs, launches, comparisons, or
   decision contexts.
5. **Briefing instructions** — what the brand must preserve, supply, or avoid so
   the creator’s advantage survives execution.
6. **Spend-protection boundary** — when the creator is the wrong hire or when an
   angle should be purchased as a test rather than treated as durable.
7. **Robustness stamp** — only when the named test actually ran.

Do not lead with methodology, creator biography, personality adjectives, or a
transcript/comments summary. Those explain the verdict; they are not the verdict.

### Commercial specificity gate

Before accepting the panel:

- Name one evidence-backed **decision-confidence role** the creator performs,
  such as reducing buying risk, narrowing choice, justifying value, reassuring a
  recent purchase, making an unfamiliar option safe to consider, clarifying a
  trade-off, validating identity, or another evidence-supported decision-
  confidence role. State the creator-specific mechanism; do not paste the same
  “purchase insurance” formulation across creators.
- If no decision-confidence role is supported, name the strongest evidenced
  alternate job — such as attention and recall, discovery, participation, or
  category debate — instead of inventing purchase influence.
- Make the verdict substitution-resistant: replacing the creator with a
  same-category peer should make the sentence false or materially weaker.
  “Trusted recommendations” and “an engaged audience” do not pass this gate.

Observed purchase questions, availability requests, or post-purchase reassurance
may support decision confidence. They do not establish creator-attributed purchase
or conversion without explicit attribution evidence.

At least one first-screen point must surface a non-obvious, evidence-backed fact
that views and likes alone cannot show. Keep the claim binding on the surface and
the raw evidence and video IDs in the drawer.

## Citation And Evidence Drawer

Every material buyer-facing sentence must bind to one or more claim IDs. The
drawer for each claim must make available:

- one to five representative evidence IDs;
- every evidence ID materially relied on;
- source video IDs and creator ID;
- capture and metric observation dates;
- support count and distinct-video count;
- evidence modality and engagement-elevation posture;
- counterevidence and contradictions;
- capture-selection and representativeness limitations;
- the derived-claim provenance required by
  `aphrodite_derived_claim_provenance_contract_v0.md`.

This is aggregate claim support. Do not create commenter profiles or imply that
one quotation alone represents the observed participant set.

## Robustness Stamp

The premium stamp is a buyer-visible receipt, not decoration. It may say that a
conclusion held after removing the top-liked admissible evidence-bearing comment
or highest-performing video only when those ablations were actually run against
the same creator evidence and the result was recorded. Removing an empty or
otherwise inference-filtered raw comment does not count as a comment ablation.

If a conclusion weakens, commercialize the uncertainty: name the angle as a
bounded test with success criteria instead of deleting it or presenting it as
durable. If no robustness run exists, omit the buyer-facing stamp; internally
record `not_stress_tested`. Never manufacture a stability claim.

## Non-Claims

- Not buyer validation, willingness-to-pay evidence, buyer proof, or commercial
  readiness.
- Not guaranteed creator performance, conversion, sales, or ROI.
- Not a demographic or platform-wide audience census.
- Not runtime, schema, storage, model/API, capture, dashboard, publishing,
  outreach, or buyer-contact authorization.
- Not a replacement for the registry’s separately owned influence,
  sponsorship, momentum, adjacency, rate, freshness, or identity panels.
