## Status and non-claims

**Status amendment 2026-07-12:** the charter reopened the canonical first paid
unit. This record remains a usable candidate motion for the five-panel Vetting
Sprint; it is no longer the exclusive first-unit or first-buyer route.
Shortlists, rankings, and other brief-specific Signals decision reads are
separate candidate units. Nothing in this amendment authorizes outreach for
this or any other unit.


`DESIGN — PHASE-0 (designed, not sent)`. This is an outreach **plan**, not a send.
It **applies** ratified doctrine (charter §3/§4/§5/§7, product-proof, the
buyer-proof grammar) and **changes none of it**, so it carries **no
`direction_change_propagation` receipt** (the handoff's test: a pure outreach
plan applying existing doctrine does not; only a change to buyer qualification,
trust-objection handling, pull grading, or kill criteria would).

Every claim here is capped at `product_learning` tier. This design is not
validation, willingness-to-pay evidence, buyer proof, or readiness, and it
authorizes **no outreach**: no messages sent, no lists uploaded to any tool, no
contact. Only the ratified holding page + waitlist are live. Outreach becomes
authorized only when the charter's own trigger fires (see §5, Sequencing).

## 0. Governing reframe — "outreach motion" ≠ the forbidden "outreach"

The charter's forbidden set (§3 moat protectors) prohibits unauthorized
Signals outreach/contact enrichment, unapproved lead-list or registry export,
and a public person-level directory. This motion is
an outreach motion. These do not collide, because they act on two different
objects:

- **Forbidden "outreach" = the _product surface_ contacting / enriching /
  exporting _creators._** That is the moat protector. Growth-map §A confirms it:
  creator outreach / contact / management is deliberately walled into a
  **separate operating/revenue lane, Aphrodite Studio** — with its own
  creator-facing authorization and neutrality firewall.
- **This motion = _Aphrodite-the-company's B2B sale_ to _brand buyers._** Selling
  a neutral Vetting Sprint to indie fragrance brands is one charter-compatible
  candidate path, not the locked first unit; this motion remains separately
  owner-gated (§4/§2). The A1 carve in the parent
  thesis (`docs/decisions/forseti_product_thesis_consumer_demand_v0.md`) makes
  Aphrodite a *sold* creator-level surface within its own forbidden set.

**The firewall this design must never cross:** the first-touch delivers a
*derived decision read to a brand*. It never delivers a creator contact list, an
exportable roster, person-level data, or an offer to contact/manage creators.
The moment a buyer wants the last of those, they want a data broker or Aphrodite
Studio — not neutral Aphrodite (handled in §4, park conditions).

*Load-bearing assumption, surfaced:* if the owner intends "outreach" to include a
creator-facing surface, this design is void and the work belongs to the Studio
lane. It is written treating the reframe above as correct, because growth-map §A
and the A1 carve both state it directly.

## 1. Who first, within the lead lane (Design Q1)

The ratified lead lane (charter §5) is indie/DTC fragrance incl. clone/dupe
houses, creator-owned DTC, and pre-designer specialty/indie. The growth-map §B
ladder is indie → challenger/scale-up DTC → big-brand displacement → VC/PE
(deferred D-5). This lane designs the **indie/challenger** first-touch; the
higher rungs are later.

**First sub-cohort: small, founder-led, creator-native DTC — the dupe/clone-adjacent
houses and creator-owned fragrance lines** whose operating model already runs on
TikTok/creator demand signal.

Decisive criteria that select this cohort over the alternatives:

| Criterion | Why it selects the creator-native sub-cohort first |
| --- | --- |
| **Decision-fit** | Their recurring decision — *which proven original / niche to chase next, and whether it is already saturating* — is exactly what the Fit panel serves (charter §5 sharpening: dupe-relationship graph + attention-weighted SoV is buyer-core for them). |
| **Reachable decision owner** | Founder-led → the founder *is* the named decision owner; no gatekeeper (unlike challenger/scale-up). |
| **Demonstration surface richness** | Their category is saturated with public dupe/review creator content, so the demonstrating first-touch (§2) can actually be impressive — thin surface = weak demo. |
| **Budget-adjacency** | They already seed creators; the seeding is visible in public disclosure markers (organic/gifted/affiliate). |
| **Likely trust posture** | Creator-native → likely `trust_open` toward creator signal, vs the craft/indie end's more probable `trust_objection`. |

- **Fast-follow:** pre-designer craft/indie houses — thinner public surface, more
  likely `trust_objection` (craft-first, "data"-skeptical). Reachable, but a
  harder first demo.
- **Next rung, still in this lane:** challenger/scale-up DTC — bigger budget, real
  allocation stakes, but a gatekept founder. Pursue after the first proofs.
- **Out of this lane (later rungs):** big-brand displacement and VC/PE diligence
  (growth-map §B; charter D-5 deferred).

**Target-pick signals (public information only):**

- Rising dupe/review creator content in the brand's category (live decision
  pressure — the niche is "hot" right now).
- Visible creator-seeding behavior (affiliate codes, gifted-PR disclosures in
  creator videos) → budget-adjacency + likely `trust_open`.
- A publicly reachable founder / decision owner.
- **Corpus-depth screen:** only target sub-niches where the registry is deep
  enough to produce a non-embarrassing fit-read. Depth-honesty is the product;
  a shallow demo contradicts the moat.

**Screen out of the first batch:** brands with no visible creator-seeding (no
budget-adjacency signal); sub-niches where the corpus is thin (cannot
demonstrate); anything that would require person-level or private data to assess.

Targeting draws on public signals + the registry's **derived reads** — never a
creator contact list, and never a list uploaded to any tool (§4, Sequencing).

## 2. The demonstrating first-touch — medium is the moat (Design Q2)

**The first-touch _is_ a miniature, public-info-only, company-personalized
fit-read** — a taste of Panel 1 (Fit evidence) plus a sliver of Panel 5
(Momentum) — not a pitch that *describes* the capability. It demonstrates by
being a decision read the founder can act on today.

**What it contains (a bounded teaser, never the paid sprint):**

1. **A framed live-decision hook**, personalized to that brand's category:
   e.g., *"Three creators are driving the current dupe-wave around [the note
   family / segment you play in] — here is who, how their audience is actually
   buying, and whether that lane is already saturating."*
2. **A 2–4 creator mini fit-read** for creators relevant to *that brand's*
   category, where **every element is a claim object**: value + provenance
   receipt (video / timestamp / quote) + freshness window + named missingness.
   Elements drawn from the ratified Fit panel: segment share of recent content
   (is this actually a fragrance creator or a general-beauty drive-by); audience
   taste from comment language (collector vs dupe-seeker — does it match the
   brand's buyer); proven adjacency (how their videos on comparable brands
   performed against their *own* baseline); niche-share trajectory / momentum
   (rising / flat / falling vs own baseline).
3. **One honest withhold, actually displayed** — the signature move:
   *"Purchase-intent read for creator B: insufficient visible comment volume
   this window — withheld, not zero."* Showing what we decline to claim is the
   trust-builder and the anti-vanity-score differentiator.
4. **An explicit boundary line:** *"This is a public-signal teaser. The paid
   Vetting Sprint adds sponsorship-load / ad-reception, aggregate
   purchase-intent, full candidate-set assembly, and a decision-owner readback.
   This teaser sells the read, not a registry transaction — no contact info."*

**What it deliberately does NOT contain** (the firewall, §0, made concrete):
no creator contact details; no unapproved list / roster / directory export
(strategic-registry boundary, growth-map §E); no person-level or private data
(hyper-personalize to the **company**, per the owner relaxation, never to a private
individual); no de-anonymized audience; no invented / zero-filled numbers; no
single vanity score; no outcome guarantee; and no offer to contact or manage
creators (that is Aphrodite Studio, not neutral Aphrodite).

**How it is delivered:** as a **self-contained decision artifact** — a short memo
/ one-pager, or a few-panel micro-read derived from that memo (mirroring the
buyer-proof packet's "memo is the substrate; any deck is derived from it") —
addressed to the named founder / decision owner and referencing their live
public situation. The delivery *channel* (a message to the publicly reachable
decision owner) is a Phase-1 execution detail: **designed here, not sent.**

**Why it _shows_ rather than _describes_:** a cold pitch asserts "we analyze
creator fit" and proves nothing. This hands the founder a read they can verify
(receipts), about creators they recognize, with an honesty (named missingness,
per-number provenance) they cannot get from a vanity dashboard. The build method
the artifact exposes — entity-resolved, receipt-stamped, missingness-honest,
niche-deep — **is** the moat (Layer-1 depth: roster judgment + ontology +
stamped derivation, charter §3). Copying the pitch therefore requires copying
the corpus. That is "the medium is the moat."

**Executable by a non-marketer** (embodying the product's epistemics): the
persuasion lives in the *artifact*, not in operator sales craft. The
qualification test is a public-signal checklist (§3), the first-touch is a
repeatable spec whose slots are filled from the registry's derived reads, and
the qualify/kill bar is mechanical (rubric grades, §3). No charisma required.

**Phase-0 boundary on the artifact itself:** this design delivers the first-touch
as a **spec + placeholder-slot skeleton**, not a real-brand worked example. A
worked example would assert un-rehearsed derived reads about a named company in a
durable doc (claim-inflation) and name-target a brand pre-authorization. The
worked example is precisely what the D-1 dress rehearsal and the first sprint
prep produce (charter §7 gate 3) — not a Phase-0 design step.

## 3. Qualify / kill bar (Design Q3)

Consumed from `.agents/workflow-overlay/product-proof.md` and the buyer-proof
packet's grammar — **not redefined here.** Per charter §5, the buyer-proof
packet's **demand-substrate hard gate does NOT transfer** (it is the parent
consumer-demand product's gate); the pull/praise, trust-objection/refusal,
kill-discipline, and graduation grammar do.

**Trust classification** (product-proof): `trust_open` / `trust_objection` /
`trust_refusal`. **Only `trust_refusal` disqualifies.** A `trust_objection`
("creator signal doesn't predict my sales") is **proof material** — capture it,
test it, read it back; the demonstrating first-touch (§2) is the instrument
built to move it. Skepticism about a specific number is objection, not refusal.

**Qualification** (adapting the buyer-proof Target Buyer minus the
non-transferring gate): a qualified target has

- a **named decision owner** (founder / head of brand) accountable for a live
  creator-spend / launch / reposition / which-original-to-chase decision;
- a **real allocation consequence** (seeding budget, launch commit, inventory
  tied to a niche bet);
- **not `trust_refusal`**;
- **public reachability** of that decision owner.

**Pull ≠ praise** (product-proof + packet): pull is paid-path behavior — asks for
the paid Vetting Sprint, schedules a decision-owner readback, a budget-owner
meeting, an urgent referral to another live decision owner, or brings a real live
decision. Praise, curiosity, "cool, send me more", "nice deck" detached from a
live decision = **not pull.**

**Graduate** (charter §5 minimum repeat/pull anchor): **≥2 independent qualified
decision owners at Grade A/B + ≥1 paid-sprint-level pull.** At sprint adaptation
the packet's repeatability / no-dashboard-no-source-system / no-bespoke-value /
non-claim criteria still have to hold (consumed, not restated).

**Park** (not kill-forever), per charter §5:

- **dry bounded batch** (no qualified live decision surfaces);
- **repeated `trust_refusal`**;
- **majority forbidden-feature pull** — buyers mostly asking for the thing the
  moat forbids: *"just give me the creator list / their contacts / a live feed /
  a dashboard."* This is **doubly load-bearing here**: it is both the park
  signal *and* the tell that the buyer wants a data broker or Aphrodite Studio,
  not neutral Aphrodite. **Never build the forbidden thing to satisfy it**;
  parking preserves the §0 firewall.

**Claim cap:** everything stays `product_learning` until receipts exist (charter
§5). A first-touch response is a learning signal — not buyer proof, not WTP
evidence.

## 4. Sequencing and the Phase-0 boundary (Design Q4)

**The trigger that opens this candidate motion** (charter §4/§7): D-1 must fire
for the five-panel Vetting Sprint and the owner must separately open this
buyer-contact lane. A waitlist-inbound signal may still pull that rehearsal
forward. D-1 does not gate every possible Signals shortlist, ranking, or
decision-read unit, and this motion authorizes none of those alternatives.
Until both Vetting-specific conditions hold, this motion remains designed-not-sent.

**Batch, not blast:**

- Qualify **~6–8 targets** in the first sub-cohort (mirroring the buyer-proof
  suggested batch); hand-build the demonstrating first-touch for the **~3–5**
  with the richest public surface.
- Each first-touch is **hand-made and singular.** A mail-merge blast would
  destroy the "medium is the moat" thesis — the whole point is a read no
  template can mass-produce. Single-operator posture: **no CRM, no automation,
  no list uploaded to any tool** (respecting both charter §9 "single-operator
  manual posture is the verified state" and the Phase-0 "no lists uploaded"
  hold).
- Run **batch → grade (A/B/C/D) → graduate or park** (§3). Do not scale outreach
  before a batch clears the graduation bar.
- **Refresh-before-readback** (charter §9 residual: no freshness SLA under the
  manual posture; refresh the read before any live readback).

**Phase-0 boundary (restated):** the deliverable is this plan + the first-touch
spec/skeleton. Nothing is sent; no lists are uploaded; no contact is made; only
the holding page + waitlist are live. The FLAG-1 commercial-use / data-rights
rider (charter §7) still carries into Phase 1 and must appear in the first
sprint's scope conversation — it does not silently discharge through this motion.
